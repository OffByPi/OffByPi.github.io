Apps I install on every Android device, in order of how often I open them.

## Caffeine

Keeps the screen awake with a single tap from the quick settings tile or a home screen widget. No timers to configure, no permissions rabbit hole.

- [Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=moe.zhs.caffeine)

## AntennaPod

Open-source podcast client. Handles subscriptions, queues, playback speed, and auto-download over Wi-Fi without an account or telemetry.

- [Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=de.danoeh.antennapod)

## KOReader

Document reader built for e-ink, with deep format support (EPUB, PDF, DjVu, CBZ) and a highly configurable UI. See [[koreader]] for setup notes.

- [F-Droid](https://f-droid.org/packages/org.koreader.launcher.fdroid/)

## Raindrop.io

Bookmark manager with tags, collections, and full-text search across saved pages. Syncs across devices and has a solid share-sheet integration on Android.

- [Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=io.raindrop.raindropio)

## LocalSend

Open-source AirDrop alternative. Sends files between devices on the same network with no account, cloud, or size limit. Also runs on Linux, macOS, Windows, and Kobo e-readers.

- [Play Store](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.localsend.localsend_app)
- [F-Droid](https://f-droid.org/packages/org.localsend.localsend_app/)
